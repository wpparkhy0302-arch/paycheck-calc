## Overview

New Jersey uses **progressive rates from 1.4% to 10.75%**. Understanding how New Jersey's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2025 Tax Rates

New Jersey uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 1.4%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 10.75%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

New Jersey provides a state standard deduction of **$1,000** for single filers and **$2,000** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in New Jersey?

Generally, any resident of New Jersey who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The New Jersey Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How New Jersey Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds New Jersey state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in New Jersey can expect to pay approximately $3,821–$7,955 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your New Jersey State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — New Jersey may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

New Jersey's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How New Jersey Compares to Neighboring States

New Jersey borders New York, Pennsylvania, Delaware. Compared to its neighbors, New Jersey's top rate of 10.75% places it among the higher-tax states in the region.

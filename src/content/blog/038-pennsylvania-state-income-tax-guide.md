## Overview

Pennsylvania uses a **flat 3.07% tax rate** on all taxable income. Understanding how Pennsylvania's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2025 Tax Rates

Pennsylvania taxes all taxable income at a flat rate of **3.07%**. This applies equally to all income levels — a $30,000 earner and a $300,000 earner pay the same percentage of their taxable income.

## Standard Deduction

Pennsylvania provides a state standard deduction of **$0** for single filers and **$0** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Pennsylvania?

Generally, any resident of Pennsylvania who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Pennsylvania Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Pennsylvania Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Pennsylvania state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Pennsylvania can expect to pay approximately $1,957–$2,303 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Pennsylvania State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Pennsylvania may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Pennsylvania's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Pennsylvania Compares to Neighboring States

Pennsylvania borders New York, New Jersey, Delaware, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Pennsylvania's flat 3.07% rate places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

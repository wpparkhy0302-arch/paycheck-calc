## Overview

Arkansas uses **progressive rates from 2% to 4.4%**. Understanding how Arkansas's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2025 Tax Rates

Arkansas uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 2%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 4.4%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Arkansas provides a state standard deduction of **$2,200** for single filers and **$4,400** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Arkansas?

Generally, any resident of Arkansas who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Arkansas Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Arkansas Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Arkansas state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Arkansas can expect to pay approximately $1,980–$3,203 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Arkansas State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Arkansas may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Arkansas's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Arkansas Compares to Neighboring States

Arkansas borders Missouri, Tennessee, Mississippi, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Arkansas's top rate of 4.4% places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

## Overview

Massachusetts uses a **flat 5% tax rate** on all taxable income. Understanding how Massachusetts's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Massachusetts taxes all taxable income at a flat rate of **5%**. This applies equally to all income levels — a $30,000 earner and a $300,000 earner pay the same percentage of their taxable income.

## Standard Deduction

Massachusetts provides a state standard deduction of **$4,400** for single filers and **$8,800** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Massachusetts?

Generally, any resident of Massachusetts who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Massachusetts Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Massachusetts Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Massachusetts state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Massachusetts can expect to pay approximately $3,001–$3,530 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Massachusetts State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Massachusetts may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Massachusetts's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Massachusetts Compares to Neighboring States

Massachusetts borders New York, Vermont, New Hampshire, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Massachusetts's flat 5% rate places it in the middle of the pack for states in the region.

## Overview

Louisiana uses **progressive rates from 1.85% to 4.25%**. Understanding how Louisiana's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2025 Tax Rates

Louisiana uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 1.85%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 4.25%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Louisiana provides a state standard deduction of **$4,500** for single filers and **$9,000** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Louisiana?

Generally, any resident of Louisiana who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Louisiana Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Louisiana Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Louisiana state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Louisiana can expect to pay approximately $1,828–$2,996 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Louisiana State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Louisiana may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Louisiana's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Louisiana Compares to Neighboring States

Louisiana borders Texas, Arkansas, Mississippi. Compared to its neighbors, Louisiana's top rate of 4.25% places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

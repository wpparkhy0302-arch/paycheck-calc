## Overview

Alabama uses **progressive rates from 2% to 5%**. Understanding how Alabama's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Alabama uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 2%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 5%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Alabama provides a state standard deduction of **$2,500** for single filers and **$7,500** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Alabama?

Generally, any resident of Alabama who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Alabama Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Alabama Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Alabama state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Alabama can expect to pay approximately $2,157–$3,625 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Alabama State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Alabama may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Alabama's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Alabama Compares to Neighboring States

Alabama borders Mississippi, Tennessee, Georgia, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Alabama's top rate of 5% places it in the middle of the pack for states in the region.

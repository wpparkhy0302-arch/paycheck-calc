## Overview

Kentucky uses a **flat 4% tax rate** on all taxable income. Understanding how Kentucky's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Kentucky taxes all taxable income at a flat rate of **4%**. This applies equally to all income levels — a $30,000 earner and a $300,000 earner pay the same percentage of their taxable income.

## Standard Deduction

Kentucky provides a state standard deduction of **$3,160** for single filers and **$3,160** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Kentucky?

Generally, any resident of Kentucky who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Kentucky Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Kentucky Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Kentucky state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Kentucky can expect to pay approximately $2,443–$2,874 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Kentucky State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Kentucky may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Kentucky's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Kentucky Compares to Neighboring States

Kentucky borders Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Kentucky's flat 4% rate places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

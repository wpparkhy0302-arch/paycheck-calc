## Overview

West Virginia uses **progressive rates from 2.36% to 5.12%**. Understanding how West Virginia's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

West Virginia uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 2.36%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 5.12%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

West Virginia provides a state standard deduction of **$2,000** for single filers and **$4,000** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in West Virginia?

Generally, any resident of West Virginia who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The West Virginia Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How West Virginia Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds West Virginia state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in West Virginia can expect to pay approximately $2,321–$3,738 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your West Virginia State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — West Virginia may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

West Virginia's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How West Virginia Compares to Neighboring States

West Virginia borders Ohio, Pennsylvania, Maryland, and others. Compared to its neighbors, West Virginia's top rate of 5.12% places it in the middle of the pack for states in the region.

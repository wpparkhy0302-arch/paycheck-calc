## Overview

Oklahoma uses **progressive rates from 0.25% to 4.75%**. Understanding how Oklahoma's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Oklahoma uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 0.25%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 4.75%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Oklahoma provides a state standard deduction of **$6,350** for single filers and **$12,700** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Oklahoma?

Generally, any resident of Oklahoma who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Oklahoma Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Oklahoma Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Oklahoma state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Oklahoma can expect to pay approximately $1,459–$3,261 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Oklahoma State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Oklahoma may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Oklahoma's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Oklahoma Compares to Neighboring States

Oklahoma borders Kansas, Missouri, Arkansas, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Oklahoma's top rate of 4.75% places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

## Overview

Hawaii uses **progressive rates from 1.4% to 11%**. Understanding how Hawaii's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2025 Tax Rates

Hawaii uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 1.4%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 11%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Hawaii provides a state standard deduction of **$2,200** for single filers and **$4,400** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Hawaii?

Generally, any resident of Hawaii who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Hawaii Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Hawaii Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Hawaii state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Hawaii can expect to pay approximately $3,837–$8,008 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Hawaii State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Hawaii may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Hawaii's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.

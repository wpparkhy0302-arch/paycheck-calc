## Overview

Colorado uses a **flat 4.4% tax rate** on all taxable income. Understanding how Colorado's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Colorado taxes all taxable income at a flat rate of **4.4%**. This applies equally to all income levels — a $30,000 earner and a $300,000 earner pay the same percentage of their taxable income.

## Standard Deduction

Colorado provides a state standard deduction of **$14,600** for single filers and **$29,200** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Colorado?

Generally, any resident of Colorado who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Colorado Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Colorado Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Colorado state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Colorado can expect to pay approximately $2,259–$2,658 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Colorado State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Colorado may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Colorado's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Colorado Compares to Neighboring States

Colorado borders Wyoming, Nebraska, Kansas, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Colorado's flat 4.4% rate places it among the lower-tax states in the region.

## Overview

New Hampshire uses **no state income tax** on wages. This means residents of New Hampshire owe no state income tax on their wages, salaries, or other earned income. You still pay federal income tax and FICA (Social Security and Medicare), but the absence of state income tax gives New Hampshire workers a significant take-home pay advantage.

## 2024 Tax Rates

New Hampshire levies **0% state income tax** on wages. There is no state income tax return to file for wages earned in New Hampshire.

## Who Must File in New Hampshire?

Because New Hampshire has no income tax on wages, residents generally do not need to file a state income tax return for wage income. There are no state withholding requirements for wages.

## How New Hampshire Tax Affects Your Paycheck

With no state income tax, your New Hampshire paycheck withholding includes only federal income tax, Social Security (6.2%), and Medicare (1.45%). On a $75,000 salary, this saves roughly $2,000–$5,000 per year compared to living in a state with a 3–7% income tax rate.

## Tips to Reduce Your New Hampshire Tax Bill

- **Maximize retirement contributions** (401k, IRA) to reduce federal taxable income
- **Use an HSA** if you have a high-deductible health plan — contributions are federal tax-free
- **Track investment income** — Washington has a capital gains tax on high earners; verify your state's rules
- **Verify residency rules** if you work remotely for an out-of-state employer

## Filing Deadlines

New Hampshire has no state income tax return to file for wages.


## How New Hampshire Compares to Neighboring States

New Hampshire borders Vermont, Maine, Massachusetts. Unlike most of its neighbors, New Hampshire has no state income tax, making it one of the most tax-friendly states in its region. Workers crossing the border for jobs in New Hampshire from higher-tax states can see a meaningful increase in take-home pay.

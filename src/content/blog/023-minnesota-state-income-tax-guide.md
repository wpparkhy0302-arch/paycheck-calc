## Overview

Minnesota uses **progressive rates from 5.35% to 9.85%**. Understanding how Minnesota's tax system works helps you accurately predict your paycheck and plan your finances throughout the year.

## 2024 Tax Rates

Minnesota uses a progressive income tax structure. Lower income is taxed at lower rates (starting at 5.35%), and higher income is taxed at higher rates (up to 9.85%). Only the income in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate — not your entire income.

## Standard Deduction

Minnesota provides a state standard deduction of **$14,575** for single filers and **$29,150** for married filing jointly. This amount is subtracted from gross income before your state tax rate is applied, lowering your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File in Minnesota?

Generally, any resident of Minnesota who earns income above the filing threshold must file a state income tax return. The Minnesota Department of Revenue sets annual filing thresholds, which roughly correspond to the standard deduction amounts.

## How Minnesota Tax Affects Your Paycheck

Your employer withholds Minnesota state income tax from each paycheck based on your W-4 equivalent form and expected annual income. On a $75,000 annual salary, a single filer in Minnesota can expect to pay approximately $3,903–$5,952 in state income taxes annually.

## Tips to Reduce Your Minnesota State Tax Bill

- **Contribute to a pre-tax 401(k)** — reduces both your federal and state taxable income
- **Maximize HSA and FSA contributions** if available through your employer
- **Claim all applicable deductions** — Minnesota may allow deductions beyond the standard deduction
- **Review your withholding** each year to avoid underpayment penalties

## Filing Deadlines

Minnesota's state income tax return is generally due on **April 15**, aligning with the federal deadline. An automatic extension to October 15 is usually available, but any tax owed must still be paid by April 15 to avoid penalties.


## How Minnesota Compares to Neighboring States

Minnesota borders Wisconsin, Iowa, South Dakota, and others. Compared to its neighbors, Minnesota's top rate of 9.85% places it among the higher-tax states in the region.
